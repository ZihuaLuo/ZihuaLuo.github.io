Finance is not only about formulas; it is also a discipline for making judgment explicit

Markets make uncertainty visible, businesses reveal trade-offs, and valuation requires a person to say what they believe about the future

## Judgment Has Inputs

Good judgment is rarely a single instinct; it is built from evidence, context, incentives, and humility about what cannot be known

That is why finance keeps pulling me toward structured thinking: every conclusion depends on assumptions, and every assumption deserves to be inspected

## A Working Standard

The standard I want to practice is simple: make the reasoning clear enough that someone else can see both the strength and the weakness of the decision
