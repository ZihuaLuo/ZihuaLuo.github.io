Risk-aware thinking is sometimes mistaken for pessimism; I see it differently

Pessimism starts with the belief that things will probably go badly; risk-aware thinking starts with the belief that outcomes have ranges, assumptions can be wrong, and downside deserves to be named before it arrives

## The Value of Naming the Downside

When a decision looks attractive, the upside story often becomes fluent; it is easy to explain why something could work, and harder, usually more useful, to ask what would make it fail

That does not mean avoiding action; it means improving the quality of action, because a better decision is not one that ignores risk, but one that understands which risks are acceptable, which can be reduced, and which would change the decision entirely

## Judgment Under Uncertainty

Finance teaches this lesson repeatedly: markets move, incentives change, data arrives late, and confidence can become expensive

Risk-aware thinking is the discipline of slowing down enough to see the structure of uncertainty; it is not about being afraid, but about being precise about what could happen and honest about what is not yet known
