Information by itself does not create clarity

The value comes from arranging information into a structure that helps reveal relationships, trade-offs, and missing pieces

## Structure Reduces Noise

When a problem feels too large, I try to divide it into drivers, assumptions, evidence, and implications

That structure does not make the answer automatic, but it gives the mind a better surface to work on

## A Personal Practice

For me, structured thinking is a way to slow down without becoming passive: define the question, map the variables, and decide what evidence would matter next
