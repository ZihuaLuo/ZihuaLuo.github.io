> Whether a rule is fair depends not only on who receives something now, but on whether it encourages future creation or competition over wealth.

Xue Zhaofeng's lecture on the manure dispute seems at first like a small legal story, but it reveals a larger economic principle: fairness is not only about who wins in the current moment. It is also about what behavior the rule will encourage in the future.

Workers collected piles of manure from the road and planned to retrieve them later. Another person found the piles unmarked, treated them as ownerless, and used them. The court had to decide who should own the manure: the people who gathered it through labor, or the person who later took it because it was not guarded.

If the manure belongs to the collectors, the rule says that labor and organization create a claim worth protecting. People will believe their effort can accumulate into property, so they will be more willing to create, collect, improve, and save.

If the manure belongs to the later taker, the signal is different: anything not marked or watched can be seized. Then people will spend more energy guarding, marking, preventing loss, and fighting over possession. Wealth creation becomes less attractive because protection costs rise.

This is the deeper connection between fairness and efficiency. Protecting labor results looks morally fair, but it also improves incentives. It reduces defensive costs and encourages people to invest effort in creation rather than extraction.

Property rights are not only protections for the rich. They give ordinary people confidence that savings, work, learning, and entrepreneurship will not be easily taken away. Strong actors can often protect themselves through power. Ordinary people need clear rules even more.

So when judging a rule, we should not only ask who gets the object today. We should ask what kind of society the rule trains people to build. Does it encourage creation or seizure? Cooperation or defense? Real fairness protects the conditions under which future wealth can keep being created.
